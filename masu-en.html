What is Masu?

A vessel that carries meaning.

The masu is a wooden cup with more than 1,300 years of history in Japan. Born as a measuring tool, it gradually became a symbol of celebration, prosperity, and new beginnings. The word "masu" also means "to grow" or "to increase" in Japanese — and so the cup has lived on as a vessel for human intention and heartfelt gesture.

Craftsmanship & Materials

Designing value from the material itself.

FOMUS masu are made from offcuts of Japanese hinoki cypress — the same timber used in architecture and fine joinery. We never fell trees for our masu. Instead, we give new life to wood that emerges from the process of building homes, transforming it into something meaningful.

The natural deodorizing and antibacterial properties of Kiso hinoki — along with the relaxing effect of phytoncide — are fully preserved. Even the shavings from production are repurposed as packing material, so the scent of hinoki greets you the moment you open the box.

FOMUS masu are sustainable by nature.
